About WOOD festival

The 3rd annual WOOD festival will be held at Braziers Park, Oxfordshire on May 21st/22nd/23rd May, wiith places limited to just 800 people.

Gates open at 3pm on Friday, May 21st & live music starts at 5 pm. On Monday May 24th the campsite must be cleared by Midday.

As well as a great line-up of acoustic and organic music, there'll be a full programme of activities and workshops for all ages - see our Workshops and Line-up pages for further details!
Workshops will run from 11 am - 1 pm & 3 pm - 5 pm both Saturday and Sunday.
The children's tent will have activities running all day both Saturday and Sunday.

Ticket Prices

Adult Ticket - £70
Teen Ticket (13-17) - £50
Child Ticket (12 and under) – FREE

Age Restrictions 

Children aged 12 and under are free. Ages 14 and under must be accompanied by a paying parent or guardian; contact details will be taken at the Gate for parents during the festival.


Three nights of camping (Friday/Saturday/Sunday) are included in the ticket price.

Not really a camper? Well why not stay in one of our beautiful yurts.

We have beautiful and cosy yurts available for hire.
All of them come ready to move into with woven bed matts, bed linen, a mirror to check your hair in the morning and other treats to make your stay that bit more comfortable.

Small yurts (sleeps 2-3) and larger yurts which sleep about 6people.

Vehicles

CAMPER VANS are welcome but space is limited, and you'll need to purchase a campervan pass when you get your ticket.

We are levying a green tax on bringing cars – and want to reward people for travelling in groups rather than bringing single cars.

Parking £10

Campervans £30 (very limited and must be booked in advance)

Public Transport

The best way to get to WOOD is by public transport - with a bus stop literally just outside the festival site, it couldn't be any easier.

Please click here for public transport travel information.

Thames Travel operate a bus service from Oxford and Reading straight to the site  www.thames-travel.co.uk bus X39 X40 X41 timetable

The nearest train station is Goring and Streatley. www.thetrainline.com

Food and Drink

The Wood cafe will again be serving local, organic and fresh-from-the-field produce, provided by  friend and renowned slow food enthusiast Will Pouget from The Vaults Cafe in Oxford.

Michael and Jim from the Cotswold Brewing Co. will be serving up their finest lagers in the Hippy arms, plus a selection of local cider and perry. Butts brewery will once again be providing finest real ale.
